You have a vision, ideas, and ambitions - but between the dream and the result sits a gap. This module gives you the bridge: **a system for turning any goal into daily executable actions**.

> The secret is not having more motivation. It is having a system that does not depend on motivation.

## The backward planning method

Inspired by GoalSumo, backward planning means **starting from the dream and working your way back to today's action**:

1. **Dream** - Where do you want to be in five years?
2. **Annual goal** - What measurable result do you want this year?
3. **Quarterly milestone** - What key step should happen this quarter?
4. **Monthly sprint** - What deliverable should ship this month?
5. **Weekly action** - What tasks belong to this week?
6. **Daily micro-action** - What do you do *right now*?

It is a zoom out, then a zoom in. You never work "in the dark" - every action stays tied to your vision.

## SMART goals

Every goal should pass the SMART test:

| Criterion | Question | Bad example | Good example |
|-----------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Specific** | What exactly is it? | "Get in shape" | "Run 3 times a week" |
| **Measurable** | How do I know it is done? | "Read more" | "Read 2 books a month" |
| **Achievable** | Is it realistic? | "Make 1M in 3 months" | "Reach 5K EUR MRR in 12 months" |
| **Relevant** | Does it support my vision? | "Learn knitting" (if irrelevant) | "Learn TypeScript" (if you are a developer) |
| **Time-bound** | What is the deadline? | "Someday..." | "Before June 30" |

## Parkinson's Law

> "Work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion."

If you give yourself three days for a one-day task, it will take three days. The solution: **set tight but realistic deadlines**. Add a 20% buffer for unexpected issues, not more.

## Breaking down big projects

A project that feels huge is usually just a project that has not been broken down properly. The rule: **no task should take more than two hours**. If it does, split it again.

Reverse planning technique:
1. Set the final delivery date
2. Work backward and identify the major milestones
3. Break each milestone into 30-minute to 2-hour tasks
4. Add a 20% buffer to each milestone
5. Put the tasks into your calendar, not just your to-do list

## Task combining

Some tasks pair well together: listening to a podcast while walking, answering emails during a commute, brainstorming in the shower. The rule: **one cognitive task plus one automatic task**, never two cognitive tasks at once.

## Project management fundamentals

Whatever the project, apply these rules:

- **Write everything down** - if it is not documented, it does not exist
- **One clear objective per project** - not three vague ones
- **A visible timeline** - deadlines should not live in your head
- **One task at a time** - multitasking destroys quality
- **Prioritize by impact** - do the work that unlocks the most value first
