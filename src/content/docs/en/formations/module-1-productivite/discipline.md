Motivation is temporary. Discipline is what remains when motivation fades. And discipline is built through habits.

## Why resolutions fail

A British study of 3,000 people found that **only 12% reached their goal 12 months later**. Why?

- No deep reason to act
- No concrete plan
- Too many changes at once
- Waiting for the "right time" (which never comes)

### A deep reason changes everything

A WordPress freelancer tells a story: his father had a heart attack. Overnight, he quit smoking - after years of failing to do so. The difference? A deep, visceral, non-negotiable reason. Without that powerful "why", resolutions stay wishes.

**The solution**: no resolutions, only **routines**. Assume you will do it for life. And find your deep reason - not "I want to get fit" but "I want to be here when my children grow up."

## Building habits that stick

### The fundamentals

1. **One habit at a time** - people who try to change several are less committed and fail more often
2. **66 days** - the average time it takes for a habit to become automatic
3. **A clear plan** - defined goal + concrete steps
4. **A tracker** - visually tracking progress strengthens commitment

### Change your environment

You are not the victim of your environment - you are its architect.

- Want to drink more water? Put a glass next to your bed
- Want to do yoga? Leave your mat visible, not at the back of a closet
- Want to avoid pastries? Change your route home
- Want less phone use? Charge it in another room

### Habit stacking

Attach a new habit to an existing one:
- After my coffee -> 5 min of journaling
- After my shower -> 10 min of stretching
- After lunch -> 15 min walk

## 6 toxic habits to replace

| Toxic habit | Replacement |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Worrying** | Meditation (10-15 min of mindful breathing) |
| **Blaming others** | Helping others - everyone makes mistakes |
| **Social media** (147 min/day on average) | Physical activity, reading, creation |
| **Complaining** | Gratitude - a daily list of what is going well |
| **Overthinking** | Action - start the day with movement |
| **Fake friends** | Mentors and authentic relationships |

## Journaling: daily mental clarity

Journaling is not a diary - it is a tool for mental clarity and self-reflection.

### Voice journaling (a powerful hack)

Every morning, start an audio recording and talk for 5-10 minutes:
- Your goals for the day
- Your challenges
- Your random thoughts

**You do not need to listen back.** The goal is to structure your thinking as soon as you wake up and clear the mental fog. Bonus: do it while walking outside.

### Written journaling

**4 daily questions** (Nicolas Cole):
1. What did I do yesterday?
2. What am I working on?
3. What is coming up?
4. What am I grateful for?

### Digital vs. paper

- **Digital**: typing speed = thought speed, easy to reorganize and search
- **Paper**: activates different areas of the brain, useful when you are stuck or need a new perspective

### Rereading old entries = time travel

Derek Sivers keeps plaintext files of his thoughts for years. The magic is not in writing - it is in rereading. Coming back to entries from 6 months ago, 1 year ago, or 3 years ago shows how much you have changed, which problems solved themselves, and which patterns you keep repeating.

**Practice**: set a reminder every 2-3 months to reread your old entries.

### Bullet Journal for entrepreneurs

A flexible analog system for capturing tasks, ideas, and information:
- **Rapid logging** with bullet symbols
- Index to find information quickly
- Monthly lists + thematic collections
- Sync with a digital tool for collaboration

**5 business uses for a Bullet Journal**: product launch plan, marketing funnel tracking, monthly finances, communication plan, weekly wrap-up.

## 11 reflection questions (Tim Ferriss)

Inspired by *Tribe of Mentors* - deep questions for a check-in every 2-3 months. Take time to answer 3 or 4 of them honestly:

- **What advice would you give your 10-years-ago self?** - often the advice you still need to hear
- **When you feel overwhelmed or unfocused, what do you do?** - identify your reset rituals
- **What failure taught you the most?** - failure is feedback, not fate
- **What belief have you changed in recent years?** - a sign of intellectual growth

These questions are not academic exercises. Write your answers in your journal - you will be surprised by how much they change from one quarter to the next.
