Productivity is not about doing more in less time. It is about **taking back control of your time and energy** so you can accomplish what truly matters to you.

> Productivity = Efficiency x Impact. Not just speed.

## What this course is not

This course is not a handbook for "working harder". It is a toolbox for **working better** - and getting your time back so you can actually live.

Real productivity is not capitalism in disguise. It serves your dreams, not your meeting calendar.

## Myths to unlearn

**Myth 1: "I dont have time"**
In reality, you have not yet decided that this belongs in your schedule. Time is created, not found.

**Myth 2: "I lack motivation"**
Motivation follows action, it does not precede it. Break the task down, start small, and let dopamine do its work.

**Myth 3: "Multitasking is efficient"**
Your brain does not do two things at once - it switches rapidly between them. The result: more time, more mistakes, less quality.

## Micro vs. macro productivity

| Level | Focus | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Macro** | Vision, goals, overall system | Planning your weekly themes |
| **Micro** | Executing individual tasks | Turning off notifications for 45 min |

You need both. Too much macro without micro = planning without results. Too much micro without macro = motion without direction.

## The 90/90/1 rule

Recommended by Robin Sharma: for **90 days**, spend the **first 90 minutes** of your workday on **your single most important project**.

No notifications. No interruptions. 100% focus.

It is simple, free, and it changes everything.

## The 3 types of focus

Productivity rests on three independent axes - you can excel at one and be mediocre at another. The important thing is to see them clearly so you can improve each one.

| Focus | What it covers |
|-------|----------------|
| **FOCUS 1 - Business optimization** | Strategy, processes, offer, customer experience, automation |
| **FOCUS 2 - Content creation** | Brand, video, podcast, social media, newsletter |
| **FOCUS 3 - Task execution** | Admin, email, outreach, network building |

These 3 axes are independent. A brilliant creator can be terrible at admin. A sharp strategist can neglect content creation. Identify your strengths and blind spots so you can build a system that compensates.

## Plan for the worst-case scenario

Planning for the worst frees up mental energy. When you have a plan B for critical situations, your brain stops ruminating and frees up bandwidth for creative work.

> "Flow is achieved best under the right amount of pressure and security."

**Areas to secure:**
- **Finances** - how many months can you survive without income? What is your survival threshold?
- **Projects** - what happens if your main client leaves? If your launch fails?
- **Meetings** - what do you do if a prospect cancels? If your demo breaks?
- **Wake-up** - what happens if you miss your morning routine?

The paradox: the more you plan for the worst, the less you stress - and the better you perform.

## Productivity as a natural system

The most effective systems already exist in nature: the golden ratio, tides, migrations. Productivity follows the same principle - **systems with processes and rules**, not individual heroics.

> "Its taking action first. Not motion, not research."

A decision made once and built into a system is worth more than a thousand decisions made on the fly. Organization means scanning instead of reading. Clean data means mistakes become visible immediately.

**Make the system yours**: customize your processes, tools, and strategies around *your* goals. What works for a YouTube creator will not work for a freelance developer. Experiment, iterate, keep what works.

## Stay available (Andre Breton)

The idea of **availability** - walking with no fixed destination, staying open to the unexpected - seems to contradict productivity. But it is a superpower for independent workers: stepping off the beaten path, exploring, and stumbling into ideas you would never have found by planning alone.

Make room in your week to explore without an objective. Not mindless scrolling - real exploration: a coffee in an unfamiliar neighborhood, a book on a topic that does not "serve" a direct goal, a conversation with someone outside your usual circle.
