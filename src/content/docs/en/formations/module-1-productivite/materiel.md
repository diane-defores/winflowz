Your work environment directly affects your concentration, energy, and performance. Here is how to optimize it.

## Your physical space

### Plants

Plants are not just decoration. According to the American Psychological Association, the most "toxic" space for humans is one with no plants and no personal photos. Plants improve air quality, reduce stress, and boost concentration.

**Tips:**
- Choose low-maintenance plants: succulents, snake plants
- Place them near your desk or in your line of sight
- Use hanging planters if space is tight

### Natural light

Working only under artificial light harms sleep quality and physical health. Expose yourself to natural light as much as possible - it is free and immediate.

### Feng Shui & layout

The layout of your space influences your energy. A few principles:
- Position your desk facing the entrance (control position)
- Match colors to the activity (blue/green for focus, yellow for creativity)
- Declutter - a clear space = a clear mind

## Mouse

Your mouse is one of the tools you use most often. Investing in a good mouse and configuring it properly makes a real difference.

### Why a gaming mouse?

- **Precision** - finer, more responsive movement
- **Custom buttons** - programmable shortcuts per app
- **Ergonomics** - designed for long sessions without fatigue
- **Durability** - higher-quality materials

### Special mention: [Swiftpoint ProPoint](https://www.swiftpoint.com/)

If you want something more distinctive than a standard gaming mouse, the **[Swiftpoint ProPoint](https://www.swiftpoint.com/)** deserves a real mention. It is built for comfort, precision, and versatility in a much more mobile form factor.

Why it stands out:
- **Compact format** - useful if you work across multiple setups or on the go
- **Strong ergonomics** - gentler on the hand than a flat standard mouse
- **Fine precision** - pleasant for detailed movements, dense navigation, and some creative tasks
- **Flexible working posture** - interesting if you want to reduce fatigue from always using the same hand position

It is not automatically the perfect universal mouse for everyone. But for some people it creates an immediate shift: less physical friction, more comfort, and a very clean sense of control.

### Windows settings (WIN + "Mouse")

- Adjust sensitivity to your comfort
- Set scroll speed (values 4 to 7 recommended)
- Enable or disable pointer precision enhancement depending on your preference
- Adjust double-click speed

### Recommended tools

| Tool | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **AutoHideMouseCursor** | Hides the cursor after a few seconds of inactivity - zero distraction |
| **AutoScroll** | Automates scrolling - ideal for long pages |
| **SmoothScroll** | Smooth scrolling animation, reduced fatigue |

## Sound & noise control

Background noise is one of the biggest concentration killers.

### Solutions

| Tool | What it does |
|------|--------------|
| [Krisp](https://krisp.ai/) | AI that removes background noise during calls (800+ supported apps, local processing, limited free tier then unlimited paid plans) |
| **ANC headset** (e.g. Bose QC) | Active noise cancellation - essential in the city |
| **Calmer** (Flare Audio) | Reduces resonance without blocking the environment - ideal for sound sensitivity |
| **Acoustic panels** | Physical treatment of the room to reduce echo |

## Screens

### Protect your eyes

- **LightBulb** - free portable app that adapts brightness and colors based on time and location (sunrise/sunset)
- **Monitorian** - control the brightness of multiple screens from one place
- **Twinkle Tray** - brightness slider in the system tray, adjustment by time or inactivity
- **AutoDarkMode** - automatically switches between light and dark themes based on time, useful for aligning your setup more closely with day/night rhythms
- **[Luciole](https://www.luciole-vision.com/luciole-en.html)** - a typeface designed to improve readability, useful if you deal with visual fatigue or want a more accessible reading experience

### Tips

- Set brightness to a comfortable level
- Use a blue light filter in the evening
- Use an anti-glare screen if you work facing a window

### Graphics tablet vs. mouse: the verdict

If you are choosing between a pen tablet and a mouse for day-to-day work: **the mouse wins for general productivity**. A graphics tablet is less precise for everyday tasks and video editing, takes more physical effort on a large screen, and lacks customizable buttons on the stylus. It is unbeatable for drawing, digital painting, and fine adjustments - but for everything else, a good ergonomic mouse is the better choice.

Typing faster means less friction between your thoughts and execution.

## Useful gadgets

### Ticktime Cube

A physical cube-shaped timer - flip it to start a countdown (1 to 60 minutes). The point: avoid touching your phone just to start a timer. Simplicity, zero distraction.

### [Supernote Tasks](https://supernote.com/)

An e-ink device such as a **[Supernote](https://supernote.com/)** can be useful if you like writing by hand but still want digital structure. The point is not to replace your whole system. The point is to create a calmer workspace for capturing, reviewing, and clarifying tasks.

This kind of tool can help if:
- you think better with a stylus than on a keyboard
- you want to reduce distraction from apps, notifications, and tabs
- you want something between a paper notebook and a classic task manager

As always, it is not a magic fix. If your system is unclear, an e-ink device will not save it. But if your workflow is already sound, it can become a strong concentration tool.

### Physical task cards

Premium paper cards for writing down your daily tasks. Writing on a "valuable" medium gives each task more weight and helps fight procrastination.
