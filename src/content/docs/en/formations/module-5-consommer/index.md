We no longer suffer from a lack of information. We suffer from a lack of **filters**, **process**, and **discipline** to avoid being overwhelmed.

> The goal is not to consume more. The goal is to consume less, but better.

## What you will learn in this module

This module helps you build a simple, realistic pipeline:

1. **Identify** the right sources instead of letting algorithms choose for you
2. **Make the web usable** with RSS, your browser, and well-configured shortcuts
3. **Handle meetings better** and reduce cognitive fatigue
4. **Break out of information overload** and get your clarity back

## The real problem

Without a consumption system:

- you read what grabs your attention, not what serves your goals
- you pile up tabs, newsletters, and videos without ever extracting value
- you bounce between research, distraction, and mental overload
- you confuse exposure to information with actual progress

## The guiding principle

Every piece of content you consume should do one of three things:

- help you solve a concrete problem
- deepen a skill you are actively developing
- feed a decision, a note, or an action downstream

If it does not do any of those things, it is probably noise.

## How to follow this module

You can read the pages in this order:

1. **Information Overload** to understand the cost of overload
2. **Web Accessible** to set up your environment
3. **Meetings** to reduce the content other people force on you
4. **Finding the Right Content** to build your information diet

## Expected outcome

By the end of this module, you should have:

- a short list of reliable sources
- a better-configured browser and RSS reader
- less passive consumption
- a more intentional way to choose, read, and reuse information
