The web is an ocean of information. Without the right tools, you spend more time searching than learning. The goal is to make the content that matters accessible in one click.

> A well-organized web is a quiet web - only what matters reaches you.

## Feed Management With RSS

RSS is the most underrated protocol on the web. It lets you follow any site without an algorithm, without ads, and without distraction.

### Why RSS Changes Everything

- **No algorithm** - you choose what you see, full stop
- **No push notifications** - you check when you want
- **Uniform format** - every site in one interface
- **No account required** - no tracking, no profiling

## Feedboard: Your Stream Dashboard

Feedboard lets you visualize all of your RSS feeds in one dashboard, organized by thematic columns.

### Recommended Setup

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| **Tech** | Developer blogs, tool changelogs, Hacker News |
| **Work** | Sites specialized in your field |
| **Inspiration** | Designers, creators, thinkers |
| **Filtered news** | 1-2 reliable sources, no more |

### Good Practices

1. **Limit yourself to 30-40 feeds max** - beyond that, you recreate information overload
2. **Review subscriptions monthly** - unsubscribe without guilt
3. **Prioritize feeds**: essential vs passive monitoring
4. **Read titles, open only what matters**

## Configure Your Browser For Efficiency

Your browser is your main work tool. Set it up like a pro.

### Essential Extensions

| Extension | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **uBlock Origin** | Ad and tracker blocker - non-negotiable |
| **Dark Reader** | Universal dark mode - visual comfort |
| **Bitwarden** | Open source password manager |
| **Tab Stash** | Bulk-save tabs for later |

### Tab Organization

- **Tab groups** by project or context (work, personal, research)
- **Pin** the 3-4 sites you use constantly
- **Close everything else** - a browser with 80 tabs is a messy desk
- **Separate profiles** for work and personal use

### Browser Choice

- **Vivaldi**: extreme customization, side panels, tab stacks, built-in feed reader
- **Firefox**: privacy-focused, powerful extensions, containers
- **Brave**: built-in blocker, fast, Chromium-based

## Fast Access To Content

### Smart Bookmarks And Shortcuts

- **Bookmarks bar**: only the 5-8 sites you use most often
- **Bookmark folders** by theme - not one endless list
- **Search keywords**: type `yt [term]` to search YouTube directly from the address bar
- **Custom start page**: a clean dashboard, not a news feed

### Optimal Workflow

1. Open your browser -> clean dashboard, no news
2. Check your RSS reader -> 15 min max
3. Open only the relevant articles in tabs
4. Read, take notes, close - do not keep tabs around for later
